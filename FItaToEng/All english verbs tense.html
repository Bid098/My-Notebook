Simple

Past Simple: Used to talk about action ended before present moment

Subject - Verb in past form - sentence

Ex. I walked a lot to the store

Present Simple: It's used:

  • to express habits, general truths, repeated actions or unchanging situations, emotions and wishes
  • To give instructions or directions
  • To express fixed arrangements, present or future
  • To express future time, after some conjunctions: after, when, before, as soon as, until

Subject - Verb in present form - sentence

Ex. I walk to the store

Future Simple: Used to talk about action or states that begin and end in the future

Subject - will - Verb in present form - sentence

Ex. I will walk to the store

Progressive

Past Progressive: To describe an action that started in the past and was interrupted by another action

Subject - was/were - Verb + ing - sentence

Ex. I was walking to the store

Present Progressive: To express a current action, an action in progress or an unfinished action

Subject - am/are/is - Verb + ing - sentence

Ex. I'm walking to the store

Future Progressive: to talk about an action in the future that overlaps or is interrupted by another, shorter action or time

Subject - will be - Verb + ing - sentence

Ex. I will be walking to the store

Perfect

Past Perfect: To make it clear that one event happened before another in the past

Subject - Had - Verb past perfect form - sentence

Ex. I had walked to the store

Present Perfect: To Express an action or situation that started in the past and continues in the present

Subject - have/has - Verb past perfect form - sentence

Ex. I have walked to the store

Future Perfect: when we are projecting ourselves forward into the future and looking back at an action that will be completed some time later than now

Subject - will have - Verb past perfect form - sentence

Ex. I will have walked to the store

Perfect Progressive

Past Perfect Progressive: to describe an action that started in the past and was still in progress when a second action started

Subject - Had been - Verb + ing - sentence

Ex. I had been walking to the store

Present Perfect Progressive: Shows that something started in the past and is continuing at the present time

Subject - have/has been - Verb + ing - sentence

Ex. I have been walking to the store

Future Perfect Progressive: for an ongoing action that will be completed at some specified time in the future

Subject - will have been - Verb + ing - sentence

Ex. I will have been walking to the store
